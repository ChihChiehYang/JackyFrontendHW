The Problem

Travelers are prone to misleading marketing from booking websites and applications that fail to meet their expectations. There are not enough real photos and reviews from real, verified travelers.


The Problem

Hotel and Itinerary website and app that shows user recommended itineraries and hotels from fellow travelers’ recommendations and user’s social connections.

User Research

Interviews & Survey

Each interviewed 5 people through 1:1 interviews and created two personas. Based survey questions around the themes discovered during the 1:1’s, to reach a larger audience.


Learnings

In depth interviews allowed us to broaden our thinking to validate and/or squash my preconceived assumptions Exploratory questions took me down new paths which helped us pivot to a new direction around itineraries.

Research for Market Fit

An indicator of a product gap in the market is seeing people trying to solve their own problems via a tool not specifically designed for the job they are trying to perform.

We saw examples of people using Facebook to seek out itinerary advice, real reviews and recommendations.

On Instagram and blog platforms there are professional travelers sharing their journeys, itineraries and photos for each travel destination they go to.

Competitive Research

Direct

Hotel/accommodation booking competitors

Facebook is the no. 1 website for community recommendations and they can be requested and responded to immediately.
Airbnb is the market leader in peer to peer rentals. It has a variety of accommodation options and clean check out experience.

Indirect

Competitors that do not provide a booking service

Being invited by friends and families to their homes relieves the pain of planning vacation details.
Google places is a highly referenced feature to see what users have to say about a location, accommodation or activity.

Research for Market Fit

Mapping

Via the affinity map help us to see the key themes to focus on for our feature prioritization.

Prioritization

Utilizing the process of disseminating features into the Moscow categories helped focus what the value of the product would be.
